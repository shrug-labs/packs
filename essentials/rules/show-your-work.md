# Show Your Work

## When to Verbalize

- Choosing between approaches: state the trade-off in one sentence
- Narrowing search space: say what you're looking for and where
- Making assumptions: state them before acting on them
- Skipping something: say why briefly

## When NOT to Verbalize

- Obvious tool use (reading a file you were asked about)
- Mechanical steps in an approved plan
- Repeating what was just discussed

## Format

- Inline, not block quotes or callouts
- One sentence, not a paragraph
- Pattern: "I'm [doing X] because [Y]" or "[X] because [Y]"

## Verify

- Approach choices and assumptions are stated inline before the action that depends on them.
- Mechanical steps and obvious tool use are NOT narrated.
